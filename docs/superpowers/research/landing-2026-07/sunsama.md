## 2. Section-by-Section Job Map

### Homepage (`sunsama.com`)

#### Section 1: Hero
- **Name:** Hero / value prop block
- **JOB:** Immediately reframe productivity from "do more" to "feel better" — position the product as emotional relief, not a tool
- **Mechanism:** Three-word triplet tagline stacked above a calm one-liner. The triplet scans like a breathing exercise. Below it, a single sentence defines the category. Then an immediate zero-risk CTA.
- **Verbatim copy:**
  - Tagline: `"Start Calm. Stay Focused. End Confident."`
  - Headline: `"Make work-life balance a reality"`
  - Subhead: `"The only task manager, calendar, and daily planner for modern professionals. Eliminate distractions, find flow, and do more high-impact work without burning out."`
  - Reinforcement line: `"The digital daily planner that helps you feel calm and stay focused."`
  - CTA: `"Try for free"` / `"Start for Free"`
  - Sub-CTA: `"14-day free trial · No credit card required"`

#### Section 2: Social Proof Bar
- **Name:** Trust banner
- **JOB:** Borrow authority from recognized names to justify the premium positioning before the user scrolls
- **Mechanism:** Named individuals with C-level titles at known companies. Short, punchy pull-quotes — not paragraphs. Titles are the proof, not the quotes.
- **Verbatim copy:**
  - `"I use Sunsama to track everything and ensure important tasks get done."` — Geoff Ralston, President, YC
  - `"Sunsama is the productivity app I've been searching for my whole life. Actually, I lied. It's better."` — Henry Ault, Founder @ Eco
  - `"The tool that has had the greatest impact on my life and my productivity is unquestionably Sunsama. These guys deserve a Nobel prize."` — Paul Galatis, CEO @ Names and Faces

#### Section 3: Problem Agitation
- **Name:** "Work is f***ing chaotic" block
- **JOB:** Validate the user's frustration and make them feel SEEN, not pitched to — create emotional buy-in before showing the product
- **Mechanism:** Profanity in the headline (unusual for SaaS) signals rawness and authenticity. Three pain-point bullets use the user's own internal monologue language. Then a calm counterpart section mirrors the structure.
- **Verbatim copy:**
  - `"Work is f***ing chaotic"` (heading)
  - `"Always on, working late, never done"`
  - `"Constantly interrupted and losing focus"`
  - `"Struggling to manage tasks and meetings"`
  - Transition: `"Sunsama turns chaos into clarity"`
  - `"End the day on time, feeling accomplished"`
  - `"Block focus time and actually protect it"`
  - `"See tasks and meetings together in one clear plan"`

#### Section 4: Features — Three-Act Day Structure
- **Name:** Daily ritual walkthrough
- **JOB:** Sell a RITUAL, not a tool — make the product feel like a daily practice with a beginning, middle, and end
- **Mechanism:** Features are organized into three temporal acts (morning/during/evening), each with an emotional outcome, not a capability description. The section header frames it as anti-burnout.
- **Section header:** `"Sunsama is purpose-built to help modern professionals do great work, without burning out"`
- **Act 1 — Morning:**
  - `"Start each day with clarity"`
  - `"Plan your day with intention by aligning your goals, prioritizing tasks, and setting a realistic workload"`
  - `"Plan what you need to get done today, without losing focus on tomorrow"`
  - `"Visualize and block time for work on your calendar"`
- **Act 2 — Midday:**
  - `"Stay focused and on track all day"`
  - `"Easily adjust your daily plan without losing focus or taking on too much"`
  - `"Pull in new tasks or events as you work"`
  - `"Mute apps to reduce distractions"`
  - `"Automatic reminders to help you take breaks and maintain energy"`
- **Act 3 — Evening:**
  - `"End each day feeling successful"`
  - `"Automatically track your daily wins, record your progress, and finish the day feeling calm and accomplished"`
  - `"See where your time actually went"`
  - `"End work on time, without guilt"`

#### Section 5: Integrations
- **Name:** Integration hub
- **JOB:** Overcome the "but I already use X" objection by positioning Sunsama as a layer ON TOP of existing tools, not a replacement
- **Mechanism:** Four categories (Calendar, Project Management, Task Management, Email/Messaging) each with a one-line job-description that emphasizes unification, not migration. The verb is always "pull in" or "bring together."
- **Verbatim copy:**
  - `"All your work tools, integrated"`
  - `"Create a single, unified list of all the work you need to do, from any of the tools you use"`
  - Calendar: `"One calendar to rule your day. View every calendar in one place and timebox tasks directly on your schedule, so you always know what fits today."`
  - Project Mgmt: `"Tame your backlog, one day at a time."`
  - Task Mgmt: `"For everything on your plate. From work projects to personal chores, bring your tasks together and plan a realistic day that fits both."`
  - Email: `"Turn communication into action."`

#### Section 6: Feature Grid / Everything Else
- **Name:** Reassurance checklist
- **JOB:** Satisfy the checkbox-scanner who wants to confirm specific features exist before committing — prevent "but does it have X?" drop-off
- **Mechanism:** Compact grid of secondary features with one-line descriptions. Includes enterprise security (SOC2) as credibility signaling.
- **Notable verbatim:** `"Everything you need, and nothing you don't"`

#### Section 7: Comparison Table
- **Name:** Competitive positioning
- **JOB:** Let the user self-select by showing Sunsama wins on the features that align with "calm" (rituals, shutdown, focus mode) while honestly showing where competitors have features Sunsama doesn't
- **Mechanism:** Feature matrix with check/X marks. Links to Wirecutter endorsement.
- **Verbatim:** `"See why Wirecutter named Sunsama the Best Scheduling Tool"`

#### Section 8: Community / Newsletter
- **Name:** Community block
- **JOB:** Convert non-buyers into the newsletter/Slack funnel — capture leads who aren't ready to trial
- **Mechanism:** Email capture + Slack community link. Newsletter positioned as content, not spam.
- **Verbatim:** `"Your weekly dose of focus and clarity. Join 300k+ people who get Sunsama meditations and productivity advice."`

#### Section 9: Footer CTA
- **Name:** Closing CTA
- **JOB:** Provide one final, calm push with an emotional outcome triplet
- **Verbatim:** `"Start planning your day. Set realistic goals. Stay focused. Go home satisfied."`
- **CTA:** `"Try for free"` + `"14-day free trial, no credit card required"`

### Pricing Page (`sunsama.com/pricing`)

#### Section 1: Trial Hero
- **JOB:** Eliminate price anxiety by leading with free trial, not cost
- **Verbatim:**
  - `"Try it for free for 2 weeks."`
  - `"No credit card required. Unlimited access to all features."`

#### Section 2: Price Cards
- **JOB:** Make the price feel small and the decision simple (one plan, one price)
- **Mechanism:** Single "Pro Plan" at $17/mo (yearly) or $22/mo (monthly). Note: pricing recently changed from $20/$25 — the current live page shows $17/$22. One plan eliminates decision fatigue. Enterprise is contact-only.
- **Verbatim:** `"One plan for busy professionals."`

#### Section 3: Pricing Manifesto
- **JOB:** Transform price objection into a VALUES conversation — make the user feel smart for paying, not ripped off
- **Mechanism:** 10 principles, each a mini-essay. This is the most unusual section on any SaaS pricing page. It pre-empts every possible objection by reframing the price as a feature.
- **Key principles (verbatim):**
  1. `"Price should be determined by value created"` — `"we chose a price of ~$1/workday"`
  2. `"Free forever plans create bad incentives, and aren't sustainable"` — `"Digital products that don't charge money, or don't charge enough, end up selling customer data or shutting down."`
  3. `"Higher prices create higher expectations, which creates a higher quality product"`
  4. `"No psychological trickery"` — `"We know that more people would convert to a paid plan if we'd price the product at $24.99 instead of $25.00 but that's just deceitful psychological trickery."`
  5. `"No lifetime deals"` — `"If we sold Sunsama as a 'lifetime deal', we'd have no incentive to improve the product"`
  6. `"Fall in love, then pay"` — `"It's wrong to build a business off of people's forgetfulness."`
  7. `"No fake sales"` — `"We don't do special deals for Black Friday or Cyber Monday."`
  8. `"Cancellation should be easy"` — `"we'll only ask you why and then immediately provide a button to cancel"`

#### Section 4: Social Proof (Tweets)
- **JOB:** Show real, timestamped, platform-native praise from identifiable humans
- **Mechanism:** Embedded tweets with dates and handles — verifiable, not curated marketing quotes

## 3. Copy Voice Teardown

### Structural Patterns

**Sentence length:** Predominantly short (5–12 words for headlines, 15–25 words for body). Never academic. Reads like a calm friend, not a marketer.

**Verb choices:** Overwhelmingly present-tense, active, everyday verbs: "plan," "stay," "end," "start," "feel," "set," "pull in," "block," "track," "see," "drag." No jargon verbs like "leverage," "optimize," "supercharge," "unlock."

**Pronoun use:** Second person "you/your" dominates (user as protagonist). First person "we/our" appears only in the Pricing Manifesto and community sections (company speaking with conviction). Product name "Sunsama" is used in third person as if it were a trusted colleague, not a brand.

**How "calm" is made credible rather than fluffy:** Sunsama never says "we will make you calm." Instead, calm is embedded as an OUTCOME of concrete actions:
- Not: "Feel calm" → Instead: "End work on time, without guilt"
- Not: "Be peaceful" → Instead: "Finish the day feeling calm and accomplished"
- Calm is always the RESULT of a specific behavior (planning, shutting down, blocking time), never an abstract promise.

### 10+ Verbatim Examples with Analysis

1. **`"Start Calm. Stay Focused. End Confident."`**
   - *Analysis:* Three two-word sentences. Temporal arc (morning→day→evening). Each pairs a verb of state with an emotional adjective. Scans like a mantra — not a feature list.

2. **`"The digital daily planner that helps you feel calm and stay focused."`**
   - *Analysis:* 13 words. "Helps you feel" — the product assists, doesn't impose. "Calm" and "focused" are parallel adjectives, both achievable states, not aspirational abstractions.

3. **`"Work is f***ing chaotic"`**
   - *Analysis:* Four words. The profanity is the mechanism — it punctures SaaS politeness and matches the user's actual inner monologue. It says "we get it" without saying "we get it."

4. **`"Sunsama turns chaos into clarity"`**
   - *Analysis:* Six words. Alliterative (chaos/clarity). The verb "turns" is mechanical and certain — not "helps you find" or "enables." Direct cause-and-effect.

5. **`"Plan your day with intention by aligning your goals, prioritizing tasks, and setting a realistic workload"`**
   - *Analysis:* "With intention" — the key Sunsama word. "Realistic" appears constantly; it's the antidote to toxic productivity. The sentence describes a process, not a feature.

6. **`"End each day feeling successful"`**
   - *Analysis:* Six words. "Feeling" — not "being." Sunsama sells the emotional state, not the objective outcome. This is crucial: they don't promise you'll accomplish everything, they promise you'll feel good about what you did.

7. **`"Easily adjust your daily plan without losing focus or taking on too much"`**
   - *Analysis:* The double negative guard ("without losing... or taking on") protects against the user's real fear: that adapting = failure. This is exactly Planevo's "reshuffles when life happens" territory.

8. **`"End work on time, without guilt"`**
   - *Analysis:* Four words + a comma clause. "Without guilt" is the actual value prop — not ending on time (a clock can do that), but the permission to stop.

9. **`"Set realistic goals. Stay focused. Go home satisfied."`**
   - *Analysis:* Three imperative sentences, each 2–4 words. Footer CTA that mirrors the hero triplet structure. "Go home satisfied" — the ultimate outcome, stated in the simplest possible language.

10. **`"It's wrong to build a business off of people's forgetfulness."`**
    - *Analysis:* From the Pricing Manifesto. A moral claim about business ethics embedded in a pricing discussion. This reframes trial-without-credit-card as a VALUES decision, not a growth hack.

11. **`"Fall in love, then pay"`**
    - *Analysis:* Five words. Inverts the typical SaaS funnel language. "Fall in love" is an emotional verb sequence from relationships, not commerce. Makes paying feel like a natural progression, not a trap.

12. **`"Your weekly dose of focus and clarity."`**
    - *Analysis:* Newsletter positioning. "Dose" — medicinal metaphor. "Focus and clarity" — the two emotional outcomes, repeated as a pair throughout the site. The newsletter is medicine, not marketing.

13. **`"The productivity app that feels like meditation"`**
    - *Analysis:* From paid ads. The simile "feels like meditation" is the distilled brand positioning. It steals meditation's emotional territory (calm, intentional, ritual) for a productivity tool.

14. **`"Tame your backlog, one day at a time."`**
    - *Analysis:* "Tame" — animalistic verb, the backlog is a wild beast. "One day at a time" — recovery language (AA cadence), embedding the daily-ritual positioning.

### Voice Summary

The Sunsama voice is: **low-volume, present-tense, outcome-anchored, emotionally specific, and morally opinionated.** It speaks like a calm therapist who also happens to know about productivity. It never hypes. It never uses superlatives (no "best," "fastest," "most powerful"). It uses the language of daily rituals, realistic expectations, and emotional outcomes. The brand is anti-hustle-culture but never says "anti-hustle-culture" — it just demonstrates it through word choices.

## 4. Pricing Psychology

### Price Structure
- **Pro Plan:** $17/mo (annual) or $22/mo (monthly) — recently reduced from $20/$25
- **Enterprise:** Contact sales (SSO, SAML, SCIM)
- **No free tier.** Period. This is the single boldest pricing decision in the productivity space.

### Risk Reversal
1. **14-day free trial** — full feature access, identical to paid
2. **No credit card required** — repeated 4x across the site (hero, pricing page, footer CTA, sub-CTA)
3. **Cancellation promise** — `"we'll only ask you why and then immediately provide a button"` — directly attacks the "gym membership trap" anxiety

### Framing Techniques
1. **$1/workday reframe:** `"we chose a price of ~$1/workday"` — makes $20/mo feel like a coffee
2. **Values-based justification:** The 10-principle Pricing Manifesto converts price defense into brand identity. Each principle is a moral stance, not an excuse.
3. **Anti-trickery positioning:** Whole-number pricing ($25 not $24.99), no Black Friday sales, no lifetime deals. Each "no" reinforces the brand's integrity.
4. **Sustainability narrative:** `"Sunsama is priced so that we can be profitable and sustainable now"` — frames the price as protecting the user's investment in the tool.
5. **One-plan simplicity:** No feature gating, no tier comparison needed. Eliminates the "am I on the right plan?" anxiety.

### What They DON'T Do
- No monthly/annual toggle that makes monthly look punitive
- No "most popular" badge manipulation
- No feature-locked tiers that create FOMO
- No countdown timers or urgency tactics
- No "save X%" callouts (they mention the annual discount but don't dramatize it)

### Testimonial-as-Price-Justification
The pricing page includes tweets and the endorsement `"Sunsama legit has changed and dramatically improved how I work everyday"` — positioning the price against life-changing impact, not feature checklists.

## 5. Three Concrete Steals, Translated

### Steal 1: The Three-Act Day Structure (Ritual Framing)

**The Job:** Sell a daily practice with a narrative arc, not a collection of features. Make the product feel like a morning-to-evening companion.

**Their Mechanism:** Three sections — "Start each day with clarity" / "Stay focused and on track all day" / "End each day feeling successful" — each with an emotional outcome headline and 3–4 capability bullets beneath. Features are organized by time-of-day, not by category.

**Planevo Translation:**
- **Morning:** "Dump everything on your plate" → show the brain-dump → calm board transformation
- **During:** "Work gets placed into real free time" → show tasks appearing in calendar gaps between classes
- **Evening:** "The plan reshuffles when life happens" → show a missed class or surprise assignment triggering automatic rearrangement
- Headlines in Planevo voice: "Start with everything. End with a plan." / "Your free time already knows what to do." / "Life happened. Your plan already moved."
- Use Fraunces serif for the emotional outcome lines, system sans for the capability bullets. Cream background with honey accent on the active "act."

**30% Rule Check:** Passes. The temporal-arc structure is a universal storytelling device, not Sunsama-owned. Planevo's acts would be fundamentally different (student-centric, calendar-gap-driven, reshuffle-focused vs. professional timeboxing), and the visual language (serif/cream/dot-grid vs. Sunsama's orange/geometric/pastel) is entirely distinct.

---

### Steal 2: Problem Validation Through Raw Honesty

**The Job:** Make the user feel SEEN before showing any product — create emotional agreement that earns the right to present a solution.

**Their Mechanism:** `"Work is f***ing chaotic"` — profanity + three pain-point bullets in the user's own internal-monologue language, followed by a calm mirror section with three resolution bullets.

**Planevo Translation:**
- **Student version:** The chaos isn't work — it's the collision of classes, assignments, social life, sleep, and a part-time job. The internal monologue is different.
- Headline options: "Your semester is a pile." / "Everything's due. Nothing's started." / "You have 47 things and 3 free hours."
- Pain bullets (student voice): "Syllabi that lie about workload" / "Group projects that eat your only free evening" / "The 2 AM panic-plan that never works"
- Resolution mirror: "A plan that sees your actual calendar" / "Work that fits between classes, not instead of sleep" / "When Tuesday explodes, Wednesday already adjusted"
- Tone: Use the same raw honesty but swap profanity for the dry, self-deprecating humor that resonates with Gen Z students. Sunsama's profanity works for burned-out professionals; students respond to wry acknowledgment.

**30% Rule Check:** Passes. The before/after mirroring structure is a universal persuasion pattern. The specific pain points, language register, humor style, and visual treatment (handwritten-style annotations on dot-grid paper vs. Sunsama's clean sans-serif) would be completely different.

---

### Steal 3: The Pricing Manifesto as Values Document

**The Job:** Transform price justification from defense into offense — make the user feel proud to pay, not resigned.

**Their Mechanism:** 10-principle manifesto on the pricing page. Each principle is a moral stance with a brief essay. Turns "why so expensive?" into "what do we believe?"

**Planevo Translation:**
- Planevo is pre-launch and likely free/freemium initially, so this steal applies to the FUTURE pricing page and to the current "why trust us?" narrative.
- **Founder's Note instead of Manifesto:** A personal letter from the founder explaining why Planevo won't sell student data, won't add social features that create comparison anxiety, and won't gamify productivity with streaks.
- **Principles adapted for students:**
  - "Your schedule is yours. We don't sell it." (data ethics)
  - "No fake urgency. No streaks. No guilt." (anti-gamification)
  - "We'll be around next semester." (sustainability, echoing Sunsama's "priced for sustainability")
  - "If it doesn't help by Thursday, don't pay." (trial promise in student-calendar terms — a school week, not 14 days)
- Place this on the About or Pricing page as an honest, slightly informal letter — matching the editorial serif voice.

**30% Rule Check:** Passes. A founder's letter with 4 student-specific principles bears no visual or structural resemblance to a 10-point corporate manifesto. The shared DNA is "use values to justify price" — which is a strategy, not a design.

## 6. Three Deliberate DON'Ts

### DON'T 1: Don't Use Executive Testimonials

**What Sunsama does:** Quotes from "President, YC" and "CEO @ Names and Faces" — title is the proof mechanism.

**Why Planevo must not copy this:** Planevo has zero users. Fabricating testimonials is explicitly forbidden by the playbook, and there are no student testimonials to use. Even if there were, quoting "Sophomore, UCLA" doesn't carry the same authority mechanism — student titles don't function as social proof the way C-suite titles do.

**What to do instead:** Use honest alternatives — a working demo, a founder's note, integration logos (Google Calendar, Canvas), and precise falsifiable claims ("syncs in under 3 seconds," "replans in one tap").

---

### DON'T 2: Don't Use "Professionals" Language or Premium-Workspace Positioning

**What Sunsama does:** Every sentence targets "modern professionals," "busy professionals," "serious workplaces." The SOC2 compliance, enterprise tier, and IT-approval language all signal corporate credibility.

**Why Planevo must not copy this:** Students are not professionals. They don't have IT teams, they don't need SOC2, and "professional-grade" signals "not for me" to a 20-year-old. Sunsama's maturity is earned over years of enterprise deployments — Planevo cannot fake this, and even attempting it would feel absurd.

**What to do instead:** Signal student-specific credibility — Canvas/LMS integration awareness, understanding of credit-hour workload, knowledge of semester rhythms. The credibility mechanism for students is "this was built for MY weird schedule," not "enterprise-approved."

---

### DON'T 3: Don't Build a Compare Page or Competitive Positioning

**What Sunsama does:** Extensive `/compare` page with side-by-side vs. Motion, Reclaim, Todoist, Akiflow, etc. Each comparison has a nuanced "use them together" framing.

**Why Planevo must not copy this:** Planevo is pre-launch with zero market presence. Comparing yourself to established tools when you have no users signals insecurity, not confidence. Sunsama earned this page through years of users asking "how are you different from X?" — Planevo hasn't been asked yet.

**What to do instead:** If competitors must be acknowledged, do it implicitly: "Not another to-do list. Not another calendar. A plan." — three negatives that position without naming. Save the comparison page for post-launch, when real users generate the "vs." queries.

## 7. Headline/Slogan Raw Material

### Headline Patterns Observed

| Pattern | Sunsama Example | Rhythm |
|---------|----------------|--------|
| **Triplet mantra** | "Start Calm. Stay Focused. End Confident." | 2-word × 3, period-separated |
| **Imperative triplet** | "Set realistic goals. Stay focused. Go home satisfied." | 3–4 word imperatives × 3 |
| **"The [category] that [emotional outcome]"** | "The digital daily planner that helps you feel calm and stay focused." | Definitional + emotional |
| **"[Verb] your [noun], [qualifier]"** | "Tame your backlog, one day at a time." | Active verb + owned noun + pace |
| **"[Outcome], without [fear]"** | "End work on time, without guilt." | Promise + negated anxiety |
| **"[Chaos word] → [Calm word]"** | "Sunsama turns chaos into clarity." | Contrast pair, alliterative |
| **"Everything you need, and nothing you don't"** | Direct quote | Completeness + restraint |
| **Profanity-as-validation** | "Work is f***ing chaotic" | Raw emotion, 4 words |
| **Temporal-emotional arc** | "Start each day with clarity" / "End each day feeling successful" | Time + feeling |

### Verb Choices That Work for "Calm"

**High-frequency Sunsama verbs:** plan, start, end, feel, stay, set, track, adjust, pull in, block, drag, mute, protect, record, tame, turn

**Verbs that convey adaptation/resilience (Planevo territory):** adjust, reshuffle, move, shift, adapt, fit, place, settle, land, rearrange

**Verbs to AVOID (hustle-culture, Sunsama avoids them too):** crush, smash, hack, optimize, maximize, unlock, supercharge, dominate, accelerate

### Candidate Slogan Directions for Planevo

Using Sunsama's patterns, translated to "a plan that adapts / calm / real availability":

**Triplet mantras:**
- "Dump it. Plan it. Live it."
- "Add everything. See what fits. Start."
- "Your pile. Your time. Your plan."

**"The [X] that [Y]" definitional:**
- "The daily planner that sees your actual schedule."
- "The planner that already knows when you're free."
- "A plan that moves when your day does."

**"[Outcome], without [fear]":**
- "A full plan, without the 2 AM panic."
- "Every assignment placed, without losing your weekend."
- "A new plan by morning, without starting over."

**Contrast pairs:**
- "From pile to plan."
- "From chaos to calendar."
- "From everything to exactly enough."

**Temporal-emotional:**
- "Wake up planned. Stay flexible. Sleep on time."
- "Monday's plan. Wednesday's reality. Still calm."
- "Dump everything Sunday. Own every weekday."

**Raw/honest (student register, no profanity):**
- "You have 47 things. You have 3 hours. Let's go."
- "Your syllabus lied about the workload. Your plan won't."
- "Everything's due. Nothing's started. (We've got you.)"

### Rhythm Notes
- Sunsama's most memorable lines are ≤6 words
- Periods between short clauses create deliberate pacing (breathing-exercise cadence)
- The best lines pair a CONCRETE action with an EMOTIONAL outcome
- "Without" clauses (without guilt, without losing focus, without burning out) are uniquely powerful — they name the fear and negate it
- The word "realistic" appears 5× on the site — it's the secret weapon that makes "calm" concrete rather than fluffy

## Appendix: Notable Omissions (What Sunsama Deliberately Does NOT Have)

1. **No demo video on the homepage.** Product is shown in static screenshots only. No autoplay, no embedded YouTube. This keeps the page calm and fast-loading.
2. **No "how many users" vanity metric.** They never say "trusted by 100,000+ professionals." The newsletter mentions "300k+ people" but this is email subscribers, not paying users. The omission is deliberate — they'd rather show named individuals than anonymous numbers.
3. **No feature comparison with check marks favoring themselves.** Their comparison table on the homepage shows competitors having features Sunsama lacks (meeting scheduling links). Honesty as positioning.
4. **No onboarding-time promise.** Unlike competitors who say "set up in 5 minutes," Sunsama never promises speed of setup. The guided daily planning ritual implies it takes time — and that the time is the point.
5. **No annual-savings badge or "most popular" tag.** The annual discount exists but isn't dramatized with percentage callouts or visual emphasis.
6. **No chatbot or live-chat widget.** Community support via Slack and email, not automated. Consistent with the "human, not mechanical" brand.
